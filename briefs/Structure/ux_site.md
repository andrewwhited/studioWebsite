# Andrew Whited — UX Site

## Purpose
This site exists to help Andrew get hired for senior, high-paying design roles. It communicates expertise, credibility, experience, and design thinking clearly and efficiently.

This is not the primary expression of Andrew's broader creative identity — that belongs to the main studio site.

---

## Relationship to Main Site
The UX site sits alongside the main site as a sibling subdomain.

- **Main site**: broader creative practice — editorial, atmospheric, expressive.
- **UX site**: narrower, more functional, hiring-focused.

The two should feel related in taste and authorship but distinct in purpose.

---

## Site Model
One-page homepage with anchored sections, plus separate child pages for selected projects and thought pieces.

---

## Navigation
Minimal top nav with anchor links:
- Work
- Thoughts

---

## Homepage Sections

1. Hero
2. About
3. Publications & Talks *(compact — biographical context, not a feature section)*
4. Work
5. Thoughts
6. Closing CTA *(not a section — page close)*

---

## Section Definitions

### 1. Hero
**Purpose** — Immediately establish who Andrew is and what kind of designer he is before any scroll.

**Content** — Positioning line: seniority + domain + type of work.

**Note** — Must communicate value in the first sentence. Name and location alone is not enough.

---

### 2. About
**Purpose** — Establish identity, perspective, and professional orientation.

**Content**
- Identity/perspective paragraph (heading weight)
- 3–4 theme/focus area blocks

**Note** — The site is present-focused. Career timeline / Background has been removed — the resume carries career history. File-icon links for LinkedIn and Resume live in the Closing CTA only.

---

### 3. Publications & Talks
**Purpose** — Biographical context completing the identity layer established in About. Patents filed. Invited to speak internationally. Readers scan and move on.

**Narrative function** — Part of the identity layer, not a proof moment. Functions like stats in a bio — answers "who is this person and what have they done" before the work examples begin.

**Content**
- IBM patent applications: title + "US Patent Application"
- Speaking engagements: talk title + venue + city/country per event
  - If a talk was given at multiple conferences, list all venues and locations
  - International geography is a signal — let the record surface it

**Design direction**
- Compact and different in visual register from Work and Thoughts
- Two columns (Publications · Talks)
- Talks entries show venue and city prominently
- Should feel like a tight, clean biographical record

---

### 4. Work
**Purpose** — Proof of capability through selected projects.

**Content** — 2–4 projects: title, summary, role/context, link to project page.

---

### 5. Thoughts
**Purpose** — Demonstrate communication, expertise, and point of view through written work.

**Content**
- Visual essays
- Adapted talk content developed into written pieces
- Each entry: title, summary, context, link to thought page

**Note** — Thoughts are written/visual pieces, not a speaking record. Some may originate from talks — the section presents the piece, not the event. The speaking record lives in Publications & Talks.

---

### 6. Closing CTA
**Purpose** — Close the page cleanly with primary exit paths.

- Quiet rule at the top, no section label, no nav item
- File-icon style links for Resume, LinkedIn, and andrewwhited.com — all three at the same scale
- Studio link uses alias-style icon to signal external destination
- No section-level padding — this is a page close, not a section

---

## Child Pages

### Project Page
- Title, role, context
- Problem · Constraints · Approach · Key Decisions · Outcome
- Text-led; avoid restricted IBM material

### Thought Page
- Title, context (essay / talk origin, year)
- Visual essay format with support for pull quotes and image blocks

---

## Case Study Guidance
Avoid publishing restricted or proprietary IBM materials. Frame work through:
- Abstracted or recreated diagrams
- Sanitized flows
- Text-led explanation
- Selective process description
- Outcome and decision logic

Emphasize thinking, framing, decisions, systems, outcomes, and design leadership rather than sensitive visual assets.

Password protection may be used as friction but should not be treated as sufficient permission to publish confidential material.

---

## Credentials Guidance
- Patent publications should be described accurately — do not present as granted patents if they are not.
- AIGA involvement should be framed honestly, especially if it reflects past rather than current participation.
- Talks may appear both as featured content under Thoughts and as compact credibility signals under Publications & Talks, depending on substance available.

---

## Tone
The UX site should feel: clear, senior, thoughtful, credible, restrained, authored.

It should not feel: startup-branded, overly corporate, decorative, vague, portfolio-template generic.

---

## Core Principle
Optimize for employability, clarity, expertise, and credibility. Be selective, concise, and strategically useful rather than expansive.

## Decisions Log

| Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-----------|
| Background/Experience removed | Duplicates About; site is present-focused; resume carries career history |
| Publications & Talks moved before Work | Biographical context completing identity layer — most visitors exit at Work; placing P&T after means they miss it |
| Publications & Talks is biographical, not a proof moment | Not "here's the evidence" — just stats. Readers scan and move on. |
| Talks kept separate from Thoughts | Not all Thoughts have associated talks; speaking record belongs with publications |
| Speaking venues + cities shown explicitly | International travel is a signal; the record communicates it without stating it |
| Links/Elsewhere replaced by Closing CTA | Three links don't earn a full section; page-close treatment is cleaner |
| Closing CTA = three file-icon links | Resume, LinkedIn, andrewwhited.com all at same scale. Studio uses alias icon. |
| Nav: Work + Thoughts | Matches content; Resume accessible via Closing CTA only |
