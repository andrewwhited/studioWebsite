# Andrew Whited — Brand Voice

## Purpose
This document defines the verbal tone for the site. The goal is to give all copy a coherent voice across Studio, Objects, Art, Image, Store, and interface text while allowing each section to adjust slightly based on its role.

The voice should feel authored and intelligent, but never overworked. It should support the visual and spatial qualities of the site rather than competing with them.

---

## Core Voice
The voice should be:

- precise
- thoughtful
- visually literate
- composed
- materially aware
- quietly assured
- culturally informed
- restrained
- specific
- authored without self-dramatizing

A useful shorthand:

- more academic than startup
- more artisan than wholesale
- more designer than craft-romantic
- more Robert Irwin than Steve Jobs

---

## Tone Levels

### Literary level
The site can be moderately literary, but controlled. It should not sound poetic for its own sake.

### Personal level
The tone can be lightly personal, especially in Studio, but should not become diaristic or overly intimate.

### Sales level
The site should be low-pressure commercially. It should present clearly rather than persuade aggressively.

---

## Writing Priorities

### 1. Precision over hype
Use exact language whenever possible. Avoid inflated claims, exaggerated marketing language, and promotional phrasing.

### 2. Thoughtfulness over performance
The writing should feel considered rather than attention-seeking. It should not sound like it is trying to impress the reader.

### 3. Material specificity over abstraction
When possible, describe things in terms of form, scale, material, light, context, and use. Prefer the actual qualities of the work over vague conceptual language.

### 4. Cultural literacy without showing off
References can appear, but they should feel natural, earned, and useful. The voice should imply discernment rather than announce it.

### 5. Restraint over flourish
The writing can be elegant, but it should not become decorative. Avoid empty poetic phrasing and unnecessary flourish.

### 6. Clarity over density
Even when the tone is thoughtful, it should remain readable. Avoid making the reader work too hard to understand the point.

### 7. Presentation over persuasion
Especially in Store and inquiry-oriented contexts, the writing should present the work clearly rather than sell aggressively.

---

## What the Voice Should Avoid
Avoid sounding:

- like a startup founder
- like a generic creative studio
- like luxury marketing copy
- like a mass-market product catalog
- overly folksy or “maker” romantic
- overly mystical
- academic in a dense or unreadable way
- inflated, self-important, or precious
- too casual
- too clever
- too corporate

Avoid:

- empty poetic vagueness
- generic artist-statement language
- faux-luxury phrases
- “perfect for your space” type product copy
- over-explaining when the work should speak for itself

---

## Section-Specific Voice

### Studio
Studio can be the most interpretive and slightly the most personal section. It should still feel composed, clear, and grounded.

Best qualities:
- thoughtful
- personal but measured
- culturally literate
- reflective without becoming essayistic

Studio is where the broader ethos of the practice can show up most directly.

### Objects
Objects should be the most design-oriented and materially specific section. The tone should emphasize form, proportion, material, structure, use, and context.

Best qualities:
- exact
- calm
- specific
- design-literate
- quietly confident

Avoid making Objects sound:
- rustic
- sentimental
- quaintly handmade
- over-theorized

The tone should feel more designed than craft-romantic.

### Art
Art can be somewhat more interpretive, but should still remain concise and controlled. It should take the work seriously without inflating it.

Best qualities:
- reflective
- composed
- exacting
- perceptive
- restrained

Avoid bloated artist-statement language, heavy art-speak, or unnecessary mystification.

### Image
Image should use minimal language. Let the images lead. Any copy should be sparse, clear, and unobtrusive.

Best qualities:
- light
- quiet
- minimal

### Store
Store copy should be clear, direct, elegant, and low-pressure. It should describe rather than advertise.

Best qualities:
- concise
- useful
- clean
- composed

Avoid:
- hype
- lifestyle fluff
- generic retail persuasion
- over-description

### UI
UI text should be short, neutral, and clear. It should never be cute or overly branded.

Best qualities:
- simple
- direct
- unobtrusive

---

## Preferred Writing Qualities

- specific rather than vague
- calm rather than excited
- exact rather than inflated
- composed rather than performative
- direct rather than salesy
- intelligent rather than trendy
- materially grounded rather than abstract
- perceptual rather than evangelical

---

## Subject Emphasis
When describing objects or works, lean toward:

- form
- scale
- material
- proportion
- light
- surface
- use
- setting
- relation
- context

Lean away from:

- self-mythology
- emotional overreach
- exaggerated claims
- generic “storytelling”
- broad lifestyle language

---

## Point of View
Use first person selectively.

- Studio can occasionally use first person where it strengthens clarity and authorship.
- Objects, Art, and Store should generally avoid overusing first person.
- In most cases, the strongest voice will come from clear, authored statements rather than heavy self-reference.

The site should feel personal in authorship, but not diaristic in tone.

---

## Practical Rule for Copy Generation
Whenever generating copy, prefer language that feels:

- measured
- exact
- culturally aware
- visually and materially grounded
- low-pressure
- structurally elegant

The voice should support the work, not compete with it.
