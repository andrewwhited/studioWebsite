# Andrew Whited — UX Site Brief

## Purpose
This site exists to help Andrew Whited get hired for senior, high-paying design roles. Its purpose is practical and professional: to communicate expertise, credibility, experience, and design thinking clearly and efficiently.

This is not the primary expression of Andrew’s broader creative identity. That role belongs to the main studio site.

---

## Relationship to the Main Site
The UX site should sit alongside the main site as a sibling subdomain.

### Main site
The main site communicates Andrew’s broader creative practice across studio, objects, art, and image. It is more editorial, atmospheric, and expressive.

### UX site
The UX site is narrower and more functional. It exists to demonstrate professional design capability, strategic thinking, and credibility in a way that supports hiring.

The two sites should feel related in taste and authorship, but they should not feel identical in purpose or execution.

---

## Primary Goal
- Help Andrew get hired

## Secondary Goals
- Show expertise in UX, product design, systems thinking, and complex digital product work
- Establish credibility through selected work, talks, publications, and experience
- Provide clear paths to resume, LinkedIn, and broader creative work

---

## Site Model
The UX site should be primarily a one-page homepage with anchored sections.

It should also include separate child pages for:
- selected projects
- selected thought pieces / talks

---

## Homepage Sections
- Hero
- About / Perspective
- Background / Experience
- Selected Work
- Thoughts
- Credentials
- Links / Elsewhere

### Homepage Notes
The homepage should function as the main orientation layer for the UX site. It should give a strong sense of who Andrew is as a designer, what kind of work he does, and why he is credible, without requiring the visitor to dig through multiple pages just to understand the basics.

The homepage should be concise, legible, and high-signal.

---

## Navigation
Navigation should be minimal and should anchor-scroll to homepage sections.

Suggested nav:
- Work
- Thoughts
- Credentials
- Links

Resume may also appear as a direct link in the nav if useful.

---

## Content Types

### Homepage
The overview and navigation hub.

### Project pages
Pages for selected work, with enough depth to show capability without exposing restricted material.

### Thought pages
Pages for essays, talks, and presentation-derived content.

---

## Homepage Section Guidance

### Hero
Should quickly establish:
- who Andrew is
- what kind of designer he is
- what kinds of problems he works on

### About / Perspective
Should communicate:
- design philosophy
- perspective
- strengths
- what Andrew is especially good at or drawn to in UX work

### Background / Experience
Should establish:
- level
- trajectory
- major roles or domains of experience
- enough context to show seniority and relevance without turning into a resume dump

### Selected Work
Should feature a small number of strong, relevant projects that link to deeper project pages.

### Thoughts
Should include essays, talks, presentation-derived pieces, or design thinking content that demonstrates communication and perspective.

### Credentials
May include:
- talks
- IBM patent publications
- AIGA involvement
- other selected recognition

This section should support the story of expertise and credibility without overwhelming the site.

### Links / Elsewhere
Should direct visitors to:
- LinkedIn
- resume
- main studio site

---

## Case Study Guidance
The site should avoid publishing restricted or proprietary IBM materials.

Even when work is discussed, it should be framed through:
- abstracted or recreated diagrams
- sanitized flows
- text-led explanation
- selective process description
- outcome and decision logic

Password protection may be used as a friction layer, but should not be treated as sufficient permission to publish confidential material.

The site should emphasize:
- thinking
- framing
- decisions
- systems
- outcomes
- design leadership

rather than relying on sensitive visual assets.

---

## Credentials Guidance
Patent publications should be described accurately and should not be presented as granted patents if they are not.

AIGA involvement should be framed honestly, especially if it reflects past involvement rather than current participation.

Talks may appear both as:
- featured content under Thoughts
- compact credibility signals under Credentials

depending on how much substance is available for each.

---

## Tone and Feel
The UX site should feel:
- clear
- senior
- thoughtful
- credible
- restrained
- authored

It should not feel:
- startup-branded
- overly corporate
- decorative
- vague
- portfolio-template generic

Relative to the main site, the UX site should be:
- simpler
- more legible
- more direct
- more obviously professional

while still retaining a sense of taste and authorship.

---

## Relationship to Main Site Links
The UX site should link to:
- LinkedIn
- resume
- the main studio site

The final “Links” or “Elsewhere” section can act as a light bridge to Andrew’s broader creative work, making it clear that the UX site is only one part of a larger practice.

The main studio site may also link back to the UX site, but the UX site should remain its own focused professional environment.

---

## Core Principle
The UX site should optimize for employability, clarity, expertise, and credibility.

It should be selective, concise, and strategically useful rather than expansive. The point is not to express everything Andrew does, but to make a strong case for why he should be hired.
